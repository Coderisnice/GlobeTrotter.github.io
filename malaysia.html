Malaysia

Historical Facts About Malaysia

  • Malacca Sultanate (1400-1511): The Malacca Sultanate was a powerful maritime empire and the birthplace of Malaysian culture, influencing the region's religion, language, and government.
  • Independence (1957): Malaysia gained independence from British rule on August 31, 1957. This day is celebrated annually as Hari Merdeka (Independence Day).
  • Formation of Malaysia (1963): Malaysia was officially formed on September 16, 1963, with the merging of Malaya, North Borneo (Sabah), Sarawak, and Singapore (which later separated in 1965).
  • Multicultural Heritage: Malaysia is known for its diverse cultural heritage, with significant influences from Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous cultures, reflected in its festivals, cuisine, and architecture.

Cultural Awareness

Malaysia's diversity reflects in its festivals such as Hari Raya, Chinese New Year, and Deepavali. Visitors should respect local customs like removing shoes before entering homes and places of worship. You should not wear pants that is shorter than your knees.

Best Season to Visit

The best time to visit Malaysia is during the dry season from March to October, particularly for outdoor activities and island visits.For places like Pulau Redang in the east coast, you only can visit from March to June due to monsoon season.

Must-Try Foods in Malaysia

  • Nasi Lemak: Malaysia's national dish, typically served with coconut rice, sambal, anchovies, boiled egg, and peanuts.
  • Roti Canai: A crispy, flaky flatbread served with curry or dhal.
  • Satay: Grilled skewers of meat served with a peanut sauce.
  • Char Kway Teow: Stir-fried flat rice noodles with shrimp, egg, bean sprouts, and Chinese sausage, all cooked in a savory soy sauce.
  • Penang Laksa: A spicy noodle soup with a rich coconut milk base, often featuring chicken, prawns, or fish.
  • Hainanese Chicken Rice: Poached chicken served with fragrant rice, accompanied by chili sauce and ginger paste.
  • Rendang: A slow-cooked dry curry, typically made with beef, simmered in coconut milk and spices until tender.
  • Cendol: A refreshing dessert made with shaved ice, coconut milk, green rice flour jelly, and palm sugar syrup.
