Maldives

Historical Facts About the Maldives

  • Early Settlements: The Maldives were first settled by people from Sri Lanka and India as early as 500 BCE. The islands have a rich history influenced by various cultures over the centuries.
  • Introduction of Islam (1153): Islam was introduced to the Maldives in 1153, and it became the state religion. Today, Islam is deeply integrated into Maldivian culture and society.
  • Independence (1965): The Maldives gained independence from British protection on July 26, 1965, a day now celebrated annually as Independence Day.
  • Tourism Development (1970s): The Maldives opened up to international tourism in the 1970s, which has since become a cornerstone of the country's economy and global identity.

Top 10 Travel Destinations in the Maldives

Malé

The capital city of the Maldives, Malé is known for its colorful buildings, bustling markets, and significant landmarks such as the Grand Friday Mosque and Malé Fish Market.

Maafushi Island

Maafushi is one of the most popular local islands, offering beautiful beaches, vibrant marine life, and a variety of water sports activities.

Hulhumalé

Hulhumalé is an artificial island known for its planned development, pristine beaches, and proximity to the capital city, Malé.

Banana Reef

One of the Maldives' most famous dive sites, Banana Reef offers stunning coral formations, diverse marine life, and crystal-clear waters, perfect for snorkeling and diving.

Veligandu Island

Veligandu Island is a serene destination known for its white sandy beaches, overwater bungalows, and tranquil ambiance, making it a perfect romantic getaway.

Addu Atoll

Addu Atoll is the southernmost atoll of the Maldives, offering a blend of local culture, unspoiled beaches, and some of the best dive spots in the country.

Baa Atoll

Baa Atoll is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, renowned for its rich marine biodiversity, including manta rays and whale sharks.

Fulhadhoo Island

Fulhadhoo is a hidden gem in the Maldives, offering secluded beaches, crystal-clear lagoons, and a peaceful atmosphere away from the tourist crowds.

Sun Island

Sun Island is famous for its lush vegetation, vibrant coral reefs, and luxurious resorts, making it a favorite among honeymooners and luxury travelers.

Hanifaru Bay

Hanifaru Bay is a world-renowned marine protected area, known for its seasonal gatherings of manta rays and whale sharks, providing an unforgettable underwater experience.
